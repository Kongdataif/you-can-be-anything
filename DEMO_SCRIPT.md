# Demo video script (target: 2:50)

Record at 1080p, hide notifications, and rehearse once before recording. Cut launcher loading, typing pauses, and silent transitions. Upload the final video to YouTube as **Public**, not Private or Unlisted if the event explicitly requires public visibility.

## Shot list and voiceover

### 0:00–0:15 — Hook

**Show:** Main menu, then select Start.

**Say:**

> You Can Be Anything is an offline Ren'Py experience that turns who you are today—and five decisions—into a personalized five-act story.

### 0:15–0:38 — Personalization

**Show:** Enter `Mina`, `INFP`, `Cinematic`, and `Curious`. Select Cyberpunk and enter the prepared opening line.

**Say:**

> I begin with a name, MBTI, preferred style, current mood, genre, and my own opening sentence. These inputs become structured context for the story rather than cosmetic profile fields.

### 0:38–1:15 — Five-act choices

**Show:** Make one selection in each act. Cut most transition time while retaining at least one full choice screen.

**Say:**

> The narrative engine follows origin, growth, crisis, climax, and resolution. Each act builds three choices from genre-specific settings, actions, and twists. My selected scene is saved into session state and carried into the developing story.

### 1:15–1:32 — Finale

**Show:** Scroll through the completed story, illustration prompt, and soundtrack plan.

**Say:**

> At the end, the game assembles the scenes I actually chose into a finale. If the AI service is unavailable, the game automatically falls back to its offline procedural engine.

### 1:32–2:02 — Cost-confirmed finale illustration

**Show:** Select the illustration button, pause on the 8-credit confirmation, approve it, then cut the waiting time and reveal the saved image.

**Say:**

> Image generation is never automatic. I explicitly approve one low-quality GPT Image 1 Mini request with a maximum cost of eight credits. The task runs in the background. Identical endings reuse the saved image for zero additional credits, duplicate requests are blocked, and failures are not automatically retried.

**Show:** Briefly reveal the playthrough archive folder containing `story.txt`, `session.json`, and `illustration.png`.

**Say:**

> Every completed cycle is saved immediately. The complete story and structured decision history are archived first, and a successful illustration is added to the same folder.

### 2:02–2:15 — Replay

**Show:** Select Try another path and reach a different first-act choice.

**Say:**

> Replay starts a fresh session, so I can change my profile, genre, opening line, or decisions and explore another path.

### 2:15–2:42 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Show:** Briefly show `StorySession`, the registered `bgm` channel, and the README judge path.

**Say:**

> I used GPT-5.6 through Codex as a coding collaborator. Codex inspected the Ren'Py architecture, fixed an unregistered BGM channel, and implemented the secure hybrid pipeline. A local proxy keeps the university credential outside the game. GPT-5.6 Luna generates choices, while GPT Image 1 Mini creates the optional finale art. Codex also implemented cost confirmation, background processing, cache reuse, duplicate prevention, and failure tests.

### 2:42–2:50 — Close

**Show:** Finale or title screen.

**Say:**

> This prototype shows how personal context and meaningful choices can make a small offline story feel like it belongs to the player.
